Abdul Kalam

Abdul Kalam, widely known as "the Missile Man of India," was a remarkable scientist, teacher, and statesman. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, a small town in Tamil Nadu, Kalam rose from humble beginnings to become one of the most respected figures in the country.Kalam's journey began as a scientist at the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO). He played a pivotal role in India's development of ballistic missiles and nuclear weapons, earning him immense admiration and recognition.

Timeline

1963

Joined the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) as a scientist and worked on the development of India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).

1980

Led the successful development of India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle, SLV-III, which placed the Rohini satellite in orbit.

1982

Directed the establishment of the Indian National Committee for Space Research (INCOSPAR), which later became the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO).

1992

Spearheaded the development of the Agni series of ballistic missiles, which played a crucial role in India's defense capabilities.

1999

Received the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, for his contributions to the field of science and technology.

2002

Elected as the 11th President of India, becoming the first scientist and the first bachelor to hold the esteemed position.

2011

Established the Abdul Kalam Vision India Movement (AKVIM), which aimed to empower and uplift rural communities by harnessing the power of technology and education.

2015

Passed away on July 27, leaving behind a lasting legacy as a scientist, statesman, and an inspirational figure for generations to come.
