Nicola Tesla

Nikola Tesla was a brilliant inventor and electrical engineer who revolutionized the field of electricity and power transmission. Born in 1856, his groundbreaking work on alternating current (AC) systems paved the way for modern electrical infrastructure. Tesla's inventions, such as the Tesla coil and AC induction motor, demonstrated his genius and set the stage for the electrification of the world. Despite facing numerous challenges and often being underappreciated during his lifetime, Tesla's visionary ideas and relentless pursuit of innovation continue to inspire generations of scientists, engineers, and inventors.

Timeline

1878

Tesla began his career in engineering by working for the Continental Edison Company in Paris, where he designed improvements to electrical equipment.

1882

Tesla moved to the United States and began working for Thomas Edison's company, the Edison Machine Works in New York City. He made significant contributions to the development of direct current (DC) systems.

1884

Tesla left Edison's company and embarked on his own independent research and inventing. He focused on developing alternating current (AC) systems and filed his first AC-related patents.

1888

Tesla demonstrated the AC induction motor, a revolutionary invention that made AC power transmission practical and efficient. This invention had a profound impact on the future of electrical power distribution.

1891

Tesla patented the Tesla coil, a high-voltage resonant transformer capable of producing extremely high voltages. This invention facilitated the development of wireless transmission of energy and information

1917

Tesla received the Edison Medal from the American Institute of Electrical Engineers (now the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers) in recognition of his outstanding contributions to electrical engineering.
